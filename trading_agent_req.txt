# Trading Agent - System Requirements (Claude Code Planning Document)
# Last updated: 2026-05-30 — all parameters finalised, Kite Connect active

================================================================================
## OVERVIEW
================================================================================

Build a trading RECOMMENDATION system (not auto-execution) for intraday cash
equity trading on Nifty 500 stocks. The system learns from 7 years of historical
data, tests on 3 years, and eventually grows into a multi-agent advisory system.

Primary goal: Minimum 3% return per month.

================================================================================
## DATA SOURCE
================================================================================

- Provider: Zerodha Kite Connect API (active, API key stored in .env)
- Granularity: 1-minute OHLCV candles
- Universe: Nifty 500 stocks (NSE)
- Historical range: 10 years — analyse 7, hold out last 3 for testing
- API constraint: historical endpoint allows max 60 days per request for
  minute data. Downloader must handle chunking, rate limiting (~3 req/sec),
  resume on failure, and per-stock progress tracking.
- Also download: Nifty 50 index + India VIX (needed for market context)
- Storage: Apache Parquet (Snappy compression) — ~5-7 GB total for 10 years
- Backup: AWS S3

### Stock Universe — Survivorship-Bias-Free
⚠ Do NOT use only today's Nifty 500 list. Must include stocks that were in
  the index historically but later delisted or removed. Using only current
  members makes backtests 20-40% too optimistic.
- Need: historical Nifty 500 composition with entry/exit dates per stock
- Source: NSE website archives

## CAPITAL & RISK PARAMETERS (all finalised)
================================================================================

  Capital:              Rs 10,00,000 (10 Lakhs)
  Max loss per trade:   Rs 20,000 (2% of capital)
  Concurrent positions: 1 (Phase 2 paper trading — single best trade per day)
  Position size max:    Rs 5,00,000 per trade (50% of capital — conservative)
  Daily loss limit:     Rs 40,000 — stop all recommendations for that day if hit
  Monthly loss limit:   Rs 1,00,000 — pause system and flag for human review
  Instruments:          Cash Equity only (NO F&O, NO futures, NO options)
  Trading style:        Intraday only — ALL positions closed before 3:15 PM IST
                        (Zerodha auto-squares at 3:20 PM; system targets 3:15 PM)
  Market:               NSE only

### Position Sizing Formula
  Position size = Min(Rs 3,33,333,  Rs 20,000 / stop_loss_pct)
  Example: 2% stop → Rs 20,000 / 0.02 = Rs 10,00,000 → capped at Rs 3,33,333
  Example: 6% stop → Rs 20,000 / 0.06 = Rs 3,33,333 → exactly at cap
  Rule: tight stops (1-3%) keep actual risk well below Rs 20,000

### Liquidity Filter
  Only trade stocks with rolling 20-day median daily turnover > Rs 50 Crore.
  This ensures a Rs 5L order is <1% of daily volume — fills without
  moving the price. Recalculate daily, not once at startup.
  Formula: min turnover = position size / 2% impact threshold × safety buffer
           = Rs 5L / 2% = Rs 25 Cr minimum → Rs 50 Cr with 2× safety buffer

### Transaction Costs (include in ALL backtests — non-negotiable)
  Brokerage:          Rs 20 per order (both legs = Rs 40 round trip)
  STT:                0.025% on sell side (intraday rule)
  Exchange charge:    0.00345% per side
  SEBI fee:           0.0001% per side
  GST:                18% on brokerage + exchange fees
  Stamp duty:         0.003% on buy side
  Slippage assumed:   0.05% per side
  Effective total:    ~Rs 496 per Rs 3.33L round trip (~0.15%)
  Break-even rule:    stock must move >0.15% in your favour to profit

## PHASE 1 — STRATEGY LEARNING (7 years of historical data)
================================================================================

Run all strategies across all 500 stocks for each trading day.
Produce one Jupyter notebook per trading day showing what happened,
which strategies fired, and what the outcome was.
Accumulate learnings into memory.md after each batch of days processed.

### Trigger
User types: "Analyse [start_date] to [end_date]" in VSCode
System processes all trading days in that range, generates notebooks,
updates memory. Saves a progress checkpoint so work can be resumed.

### Strategies to Implement and Backtest (22 total)

  BREAKOUT / MOMENTUM:
  1.  ORB-15: Opening Range Breakout using first 15-min high/low as range.
              Trade breakout after 9:30 AM with volume confirmation.
  2.  ORB-30: Same as ORB-15 but using 30-min range (fewer, more reliable signals).
  3.  PDH/PDL: Price crosses previous day's high or low with rising volume.
  4.  Gap continuation: stock gaps up >1%, buy first pullback to VWAP within 30 min.
  5.  Volume spike breakout: price at resistance + volume > 2× 20-day average.

  MEAN REVERSION:
  6.  VWAP reversion: price >1.5% from VWAP, trade back toward VWAP.
                      Stop beyond the deviation extreme.
  7.  RSI extremes: RSI(14) on 5-min chart < 25 → buy, > 75 → sell signal.
  8.  Bollinger Band reversion: price at outer band + RSI confirms → trade to midline.
  9.  Gap fade: fade gaps >3% that historically fill intraday.

  TREND FOLLOWING:
  10. EMA crossover: 9 EMA crosses 21 EMA on 5-min chart, confirmed by price
                     above VWAP and volume above average.
  11. Supertrend (10, 3): direction flip on 5-min or 15-min chart.
  12. MACD crossover: signal line cross with histogram flip on 15-min chart.

  PRICE ACTION:
  13. Support/resistance breakout: key intraday level break with volume.
  14. NR7 / Inside Day: narrow range setup leading to next-day explosive open.
  15. First 5-min candle: buy above / sell below the first candle of the day.

  PIVOT-BASED (critical for Indian intraday — widely used by professionals):
  16. CPR (Central Pivot Range):
      Pivot = (H+L+C)/3,  BC = (H+L)/2,  TC = Pivot + (Pivot - BC)
      Narrow TC-BC (<0.3%) → trending day → use breakouts
      Wide TC-BC (>0.8%)   → sideways day → use reversions
      Signal: price holds above TC = bullish, below BC = bearish bias.
      Entry on first pullback to TC/BC after 9:30 AM.

  17. Camarilla Pivots:
      H3 = C + (H-L)×1.1/4    L3 = C - (H-L)×1.1/4
      H4 = C + (H-L)×1.1/2    L4 = C - (H-L)×1.1/2
      Reversal: price at L3 → buy, stop below L4, target H3.
      Breakout: price breaks H4 → momentum long.

  VOLATILITY / INSTITUTIONAL:
  18. ADX filter (overlay, not standalone):
      ADX > 25 + DI+ > DI- → strong trend → favour breakouts, avoid reversions.
      ADX < 20 → sideways → favour VWAP/CPR reversions.
      Applied as a condition modifier on top of other strategies.

  19. VWAP Standard Deviation Bands:
      Compute rolling VWAP ± 1σ and ± 2σ from market open each day.
      Price at -2σ + RSI(5) < 25 → strong institutional support → buy.
      Price at +2σ + RSI(5) > 75 → overbought → short candidate.
      Exit at VWAP (0σ). More reliable than basic VWAP reversion.

  MOMENTUM OSCILLATORS:
  20. Stochastic crossover (5-min):
      %K crosses above %D from below 20 → buy (oversold reversal).
      %K crosses below %D from above 80 → sell.
      Only valid if price direction agrees with VWAP position.

  21. Volume Profile / VPOC:
      VPOC = price level with highest traded volume (strongest S/R).
      VAH/VAL = top and bottom of 70% volume area.
      Price rejects VPOC on first test → reversion trade.
      Price breaks above VAH with volume → continuation.
      Use prior day's profile for pre-market level setting.

  MARKET RELATIVE:
  22. Relative strength: stock outperforming Nifty 50 in first 30 min
                         → momentum continuation. Sector leader bonus.

  COMPOSITE SCORING:
  - After running all strategies, compute a weighted composite score per stock.
  - Weights are learned from backtested performance (not hand-tuned).
  - Top 3 positive-scoring stocks = recommendations for that day.

### Minimum Trade Quality Filter (all conditions must be true to recommend)
  1. Liquidity: avg 20-day turnover > Rs 20 Crore
  2. Risk:Reward ≥ 1.5 (target at least 1.5× the stop distance)
  3. At least 2 independent strategies agree on direction
  4. Volume: current > 1.5× same-time-yesterday volume
  5. Not in upper/lower circuit limit
  6. Time: no new entries after 2:00 PM IST

### Daily Notebook Contents (one notebook per trading day)
  a) Market context: Nifty 50 direction, India VIX, gap from prev close,
                     global cues (SGX Nifty, Dow futures, crude)
  b) Master DataFrame: one row per stock with all strategy signals,
                       entry/target/stop per strategy, composite score, rank
  c) Top 3 recommendations: entry zone, target, stop, position size, reasoning
  d) Post-market review: what worked, what failed, and why
  e) Rolling strategy leaderboard: cumulative win rate per strategy

### 7-Year Learning — How It Works
  Process each trading day SEQUENTIALLY in date order, simulating a live system.
  Use only data available before each day — zero look-ahead bias.
  Since outcomes are known (historical data), analysis and evaluation happen
  simultaneously — no separate test needed within the 7-year window.
  At year-end: write yearly summary to memory.md and update strategy weights.
  After all 7 years: freeze weights → 3-year holdout test begins.
  See how.txt for implementation details.

### Adaptive Strategy Weighting (Unlearning)
  The system must continuously track recent performance of each strategy and
  adjust its contribution to the composite score automatically:
  - Strategies failing recent recommendations → downweighted
  - Strategies working well recently → upweighted
  - No strategy is ever fully removed (markets are cyclical)
  - Current market regime (VIX, trend strength) also modifies weights daily
  - A suppressed strategy auto-revives when its regime returns
  - All weight changes logged to memory.md with date and reason
  See how.txt for the exact weight formula and regime override rules.

### Memory System (memory.md — updated after every analysis session)
  - Current strategy weights and trend (rising/falling/stable)
  - Strategy performance: rolling win rate, avg return, best/worst conditions
  - Yearly summaries: market regime, what worked, what did not
  - Stock behaviour patterns: recurring intraday tendencies per stock
  - Market regime guide: which strategies work in which conditions
  - Lessons learned: numbered, most recent first

## PHASE 2 — TESTING (2023–2026, frozen weights, strict forward-only)
================================================================================

### Core Rules — Non-Negotiable

  1. STRICTLY FORWARD ONLY
     Never look at future candle data. For any trade_date D, the engine
     may only use candles with datetime < D at 9:15 AM. No peeking at
     today's open, yesterday's close after the bell, or any data from D+1.
     This is already enforced by history_5min = df[df.date < trade_date].
     Do NOT relax this for any reason during testing.

  2. ONE TRADE PER DAY
     MAX_RECOMMENDATIONS = 1 for testing years (changed from 3).
     Pick only the single highest-confidence trade per day — the stock
     with the highest composite score that passes all quality filters.
     Reason: 1 trade forces the system to commit to its best call.
     Testing with 3 dilutes accountability and masks weak decisions.

  3. FROZEN WEIGHTS
     Weights learned during 2016–2022 are frozen at the end of 2022.
     No weight updates during testing years. This proves the edge is
     real and not a result of the system continually fitting to new data.

  4. INCLUDE ALL TRANSACTION COSTS
     Same cost model as learning phase. Slippage 0.05% per side minimum.
     The test P&L must be net of all costs — no exceptions.

### Strategy Inclusion — Which Strategies Count

  The composite scorer runs ALL 27 strategies every day (weights are
  frozen, not zeroed — suppressed strategies contribute weight×0.1).

  For the QUALITY FILTER "min 2 strategies agree" check:
  ONLY count a strategy toward the agreement count if it had
  win% >= 50% in at least one year during 2016–2022.
  Rationale: a suppressed strategy with win% 29% that happens to fire
  in the same direction should not "count" as an independent agreement.
  Strategies with verified historical win% >= 50% represent real edge.

  Strategies that qualify (win% >= 50% in 1+ learning year):
    VOL-SPIKE, VWAP-REV, RSI-EXT, GAP-CONT, GAP-FADE, ADX-FILTER,
    ORB-15, ORB-30, PDH-PDL, CPR, REL-STR, BOLLINGER, MACD, SR-BREAK,
    FIRST-CANDLE, CAMARILLA, VPOC, SUPERTREND, DBL-BTM, ASC-TRI,
    FALL-WEDGE, BULL-FLAG, DAILY-BIAS, STOCHASTIC, VWAP-STDDEV

  Excluded from agreement count (never reached 50% win rate in any year):
    EMA-CROSS, NR7 (no signals in any year)

  Note on suppressed-but-high-winrate strategies (e.g. VWAP-REV):
  VWAP-REV weight was 0.19 at end of 2022 (suppressed by recent form),
  but its lifetime win% was 54–77% in 2017–2020. It still participates
  in composite scoring at weight 0.19 AND counts toward agreement because
  it cleared 50% win% historically. The weight handles magnitude;
  the agreement filter handles statistical credibility.

### Paper Trade DataFrame — Record Every Trade

  One row per trade. Append to data/trade_logs/paper_trades.csv.
  Use CSV (not parquet) — human-readable, easy to open in Excel/Sheets,
  easy to inspect and manually annotate after each trading session.

  Columns (in order):
  +-----------------------+---------------------------------------------------+
  | date                  | Trading date (YYYY-MM-DD)                         |
  | symbol                | Stock symbol e.g. RELIANCE                        |
  | signal_time           | Time signal fired e.g. "10:30"                    |
  | entry_price           | Price at which system recommends buying           |
  | quantity              | Number of shares (from position sizing formula)   |
  | position_rs           | Total capital deployed (entry × quantity)         |
  | stop_loss             | Stop loss price                                   |
  | target                | Target price (profit objective)                   |
  | rr                    | Risk:reward ratio at entry                        |
  | strategies_fired      | Comma-separated list of all strategies that voted |
  |                       | e.g. "VOL-SPIKE, VWAP-REV, RSI-EXT"              |
  | agreeing_count        | Number of qualifying (>=50% win rate) strategies  |
  |                       | that agreed on direction                          |
  | composite_score       | Weighted composite score at time of signal        |
  | driver_strategy       | Strategy with highest RR (used for entry/target/  |
  |                       | stop prices)                                      |
  | reason                | One-line reason e.g. "VOL-SPIKE: volume 3x at    |
  |                       | resistance 1450; RSI-EXT: RSI=22 oversold"        |
  | exit_time             | Time position was closed ("15:15" if time exit)   |
  | exit_price            | Actual exit price                                 |
  | exit_reason           | TARGET_HIT / STOP_HIT / TIME_EXIT                 |
  | result                | EXACT_WIN / WIN / LOSS                            |
  | predicted_win_pct     | Estimated probability of win at entry time:       |
  |                       | weighted avg of win% of strategies that fired,    |
  |                       | weighted by their frozen weight. E.g. 74.5%       |
  | pnl_rs                | Net P&L in rupees after all transaction costs     |
  | pnl_pct               | Net P&L as % of entry capital                     |
  +-----------------------+---------------------------------------------------+

  Result definitions (important — applied exactly this way):
    EXACT_WIN:  exit_price >= target price          (system called it right)
    WIN:        exit_price > entry_price but        (profitable but target
                exit_price < target                  not reached — time exit)
    LOSS:       exit_price <= entry_price            (stopped out or time exit
                                                     at or below entry)

  Note: WIN (without EXACT) = closed profitably via TIME_EXIT before target.
  The system's prediction was directionally correct but the move didn't
  complete in the trading session. Still counts as a win for capital.

### Testing Performance Targets

  Monthly return:       >= 3%  (primary target)
  Win rate (EXACT_WIN): >= 35%
  Effective win rate    >= 55%  (EXACT_WIN + WIN combined)
  Max daily drawdown:   <= Rs 20,000 (2% of capital)
  Max monthly loss:     <= Rs 1,00,000
  Profit factor:        >= 1.5  (total wins / total losses)

### Per-Strategy Report (generated at end of each testing year)
  Win rate (exact), effective win rate, total signals, P&L contribution,
  avg win Rs, avg loss Rs, profit factor, best month, worst month.

### Portfolio Report
  Monthly return series, comparison vs Nifty 50 buy-and-hold,
  worst consecutive losing streak, Sharpe ratio on daily P&L.

## PHASE 2.5 — PRE-LIVE VALIDATION (before any real money is deployed)
================================================================================

The backtest and simulation tell you the strategy has an edge — that is
valuable. What they do NOT tell you is the exact size of that edge after
real-world execution friction. This phase bridges the gap.

### Known Gaps Between Simulation and Live Market

  1. EXECUTION DELAY (most critical)
     Simulation:  5-min candle closes at 10:30 → signal fires → entry at
                  10:30 close price.
     Live market: candle closes at 10:30 → code runs → API call → order
                  placed at ~10:30:03. Price has already moved.
     Impact:      In fast-moving breakouts (ORB, VOL-SPIKE), 3 seconds can
                  mean 0.3–0.5% extra slippage beyond the 0.05% modelled.

  2. SURVIVORSHIP BIAS IN UNIVERSE
     The Nifty 500 list used is TODAY's — companies that survived and grew.
     In 2016, many of these stocks were smaller or not yet listed.
     The backtest unknowingly favours stocks that "turned out fine."
     Fix: use historical Nifty 500 composition with entry/exit dates.
          (noted in DATA SOURCE section above — still outstanding)

  3. DATA SNOOPING / STRATEGY SELECTION BIAS
     The 27 strategies were chosen by humans who already knew what patterns
     tend to work in equity markets. This bakes in an optimistic view.
     A strategy that "worked in backtest" partly works because it was
     selected with prior knowledge.
     Mitigation: the 3-year frozen-weight test (2023–2026) is the real
                 indicator — if P&L holds on truly unseen data, the edge
                 is genuine.

  4. PARTIAL FILLS AND ORDER REJECTION
     Simulation:  assumes 100% fill at signal price, always.
     Live market: a Rs 3.33L order in a mid-cap may fill 60% at target
                  price, rest at a worse price — or get rejected during
                  circuit/high-volatility periods.

  5. API RATE LIMITS IN LIVE MODE
     Simulation:  processes 500 stocks in memory in seconds.
     Live market: Kite WebSocket delivers live ticks; processing 500
                  stocks, scoring, and filtering takes clock time — the
                  opportunity window may have partially closed.

  6. LOOK-AHEAD IN CANDLE ENTRY PRICE
     Simulation:  uses 5-min candle close as entry price (known only at
                  candle end).
     Live market: order goes in at 10:30:02 — i.e., the OPEN of the next
                  candle (10:30–10:35), which can be a different price
                  depending on market speed.

### Required Steps Before Deploying Real Capital

  STEP 1 — PAPER TRADING (3–6 months minimum)
    - Build a live data pipeline: replace read_parquet() with a Kite
      WebSocket feed that builds 5-min candles in real time as they close.
    - Run the full signal engine on live data every day at 9:15 AM.
    - Log every signal, entry, target, stop — but place NO actual orders.
    - At end of day, record what price was actually achievable vs simulated.
    - Goal: measure the real execution gap (expected ~0.1–0.5% per trade).

  STEP 2 — EXECUTION AUDIT
    - After paper trading, calculate average slippage per strategy type.
    - Update the cost model in config/settings.py with real measured
      slippage instead of the assumed 0.05%.
    - Strategies whose edge disappears after real slippage → remove or
      retune before going live.

  STEP 3 — LIVE DATA PIPELINE
    Build the following components (does not exist yet):
    a) Kite WebSocket subscriber — receives live ticks for all 500 stocks.
    b) 5-min candle builder — aggregates ticks into OHLCV candles.
    c) Signal engine runner — fires strategies as each candle closes.
    d) Signal logger — writes signals to file/DB with timestamp.
    This replaces the overnight batch processing model entirely.

  STEP 4 — ORDER MANAGEMENT LAYER
    Build before live trading (does not exist yet):
    a) kite.place_order() wrapper with retry on rejection.
    b) Position tracker — knows what is currently open.
    c) Stop loss monitor — watches open positions and exits on stop hit.
    d) Square-off job — closes all positions at 3:15 PM IST automatically.
    e) Daily loss circuit breaker — halts new orders if Rs 40,000 hit.

  STEP 5 — SMALL CAPITAL LIVE TEST (2–3 months)
    - Deploy with 10% of capital (Rs 1,00,000) only.
    - Max position size: Rs 33,333 (scaled down proportionally).
    - Run for 2–3 months — compare actual P&L vs paper trading P&L.
    - If actual P&L is within 70% of paper P&L → scale up to full capital.
    - If actual P&L is <50% of paper P&L → diagnose before scaling.

### Performance Gate to Full Deployment
  Paper trading  : P&L positive for 3 consecutive months
  Small capital  : actual P&L ≥ 70% of paper P&L for 2 months
  Max drawdown   : does not breach Rs 15,000 (15% of Rs 1L test capital)
  Execution gap  : measured slippage < 0.3% per trade on average
  Only after all 4 gates pass → deploy full Rs 10,00,000 capital.

### Recommended Sequence
  Finish 2019 → 2022 analysis (learning phase)
  → Check 2023–2026 testing P&L on frozen weights
  → If edge holds → begin paper trading on live Kite WebSocket feed
  → 3–6 months paper trading → execution audit → update cost model
  → 2–3 months small capital live test
  → Full deployment with multi-agent system (Phase 3)

## PHASE 2.6 — MONITORING DASHBOARD (Streamlit, build alongside Phase 2.5)
================================================================================

Before the multi-agent system, we need a local visual monitoring layer so we
can inspect every trade, track strategy performance, and watch live signals —
without manually opening CSV/parquet files.

### Technology: Streamlit (chosen over Dash / Grafana / Panel)

  Why Streamlit:
  - Reads our parquet/CSV files directly — zero adapter code
  - Pure Python — no JS frontend, no separate server, no Docker
  - Plotly already in requirements → every chart type available
  - Multi-page layout (v1.35+) gives clean navigation
  - Auto-refresh via st.rerun() for live monitoring
  - Runs alongside run_live.py safely (read-only access)

  Why NOT TradingView:
  - TradingView shows market data — it cannot read our local trade logs,
    strategy weights, composite scores, or agent checkpoints.
  - Our monitoring need is about OUR system's output, not price charts.
  - TradingView candlestick widget CAN be embedded inside our Streamlit
    pages as a complement for price visualization on individual stocks.

  Run command:
    .\venv\Scripts\streamlit.exe run dashboard\app.py --server.port 8501
  Opens: http://localhost:8501 (auto-launched in browser)

  New dependency: streamlit>=1.35.0  (add to requirements.txt)

### WHY SHORT SELLING

The current system is long-only cash equity. All 22 strategies already generate
direction=-1 (sell) signals — the engine logs them but never acts on them.
This is leaving half the edge on the table.

Indian intraday short selling in cash equity (NSE) is legal and practical:
  - SEBI has allowed short selling since 2008
  - MIS order type at Zerodha: sell first, buy back before 3:20 PM auto-square-off
  - Same Rs 10,00,000 capital, same 1-trade-per-day rule, same instruments
  - Transaction costs identical to long side (STT applies to the opening sell leg)
  - The cost_model.py net_pnl(direction=-1) is already implemented correctly

Why it adds real value:
  - Demonetization 2016: Nifty fell 8% in 3 days. Buy-side would have been stopped out
    every day. Short-side would have captured the fall.
  - COVID crash Feb–March 2020: Nifty dropped 38% in 6 weeks. The learning phase
    (2016–2022) includes this. Short strategies learn when to press hard.
  - 2022 bear market (Fed rate hikes): 6 months of slow grind down. Perfectly suited
    for mean-reversion shorts (VWAP-REV, RSI-EXT overbought side).
  - In any given month in a bull market, 40–60% of Nifty 500 stocks fall on any given day.
    Short selling is not only for bear markets.

Expected contribution: 20–30% of total P&L, concentrated during high-VIX periods.
Short win rate typically lower (35–45%) but larger average profit vs loss.

### ARCHITECTURE DECISION — ONE APP, NOT TWO

DO NOT create a separate app for short selling. Reason:

  A separate app means:
  - Duplicate data pipeline reading the same 500 Nifty 500 stock files
  - Two versions of the backtester engine to maintain
  - Two dashboards to check each morning
  - Two sets of quality filters to update when SEBI rules change
  - Any bug fix must be applied in two places

  The unified approach:
  - composite_scorer computes long_score (from +1 signals) and short_score (from -1 signals)
  - Engine picks the trade with highest confidence: max(long_score, |short_score|)
  - paper_trades.csv gets one new column: direction = LONG / SHORT
  - Dashboard gets one new dropdown: Direction = All / LONG / SHORT
  - All existing strategy weights, quality filters, cost model reused

  The composite scorer's sign already encodes direction naturally.
  Positive composite = bullish consensus. Negative composite = bearish consensus.
  Forcing them into two apps would artificially split what is one unified signal.

### SHORT-SIDE STRATEGY SELECTION

The 22 existing strategies already generate -1 signals for the short side.
But there are bearish patterns that have NO buy-side mirror and that the
existing strategies will never identify. For completeness, add 8 new bearish
patterns specifically for the short side:

  BEARISH REVERSAL PATTERNS (mirrors of existing bullish chart patterns):
  S1.  DBL-TOP: M-shaped pattern — two touches of resistance with valley between.
               Short on close below neckline (valley low) with volume.
               Mirror of existing DBL-BTM.

  S2.  DESC-TRI: Descending triangle — lower highs touching flat support.
                Short on breakdown below support with volume expansion.
                Mirror of existing ASC-TRI.

  S3.  RISE-WEDGE: Rising wedge — both highs and lows rising but converging.
                  Breakout bias is downward. Short on break below lower trendline.
                  Mirror of existing FALL-WEDGE (which is bullish reversal).

  S4.  BEAR-FLAG: Sharp impulsive drop of 2–4% in 3–5 bars, then tight
                 parallel consolidation on declining volume (flag).
                 Short on breakdown out of the flag. Mirror of BULL-FLAG.

  BEARISH SETUPS WITH NO BUY-SIDE MIRROR:
  S5.  FAILED-BO (Failed Breakout / Bull Trap):
       Price breaks above a key resistance (PDH, ORB high, round number) and
       TRIGGERS buy signals from ORB/SR-BREAK/VOL-SPIKE, BUT closes back below
       the resistance within 1–2 bars on above-average volume.
       This is the single most reliable intraday short signal.
       Logic: the trapped longs are now panic sellers; ride their stops.
       Identification: price > resistance for 1 bar then close < resistance
                       + volume spike on reversal bar.

  S6.  DEAD-CAT:
       Stock has gapped down >3% at open. During the session, it bounces 1–2%
       toward VWAP on declining volume. The bounce loses steam before reaching
       VWAP. Short on the first bar where price starts declining from the bounce
       high with volume below the gap-down bar's volume.
       Target: morning's gap-down low. Stop: VWAP.
       Logic: institutional sellers use the bounce to exit; retail buyers
              who bought the dip become the fuel for the second leg down.

  S7.  OPEN-WEAK:
       Stock opens within 0.5% of its previous day's low AND the first 15-min
       candle (9:15–9:30) makes a new low AND Nifty 50 is also down >0.3%.
       Short immediately after first 15-min close, stop above the opening price.
       Logic: stocks that cannot hold the previous day's low on a weak market
              open tend to fall throughout the day — sustained institutional
              selling from pre-market, not just panic.

  S8.  BEAR-ENGULF:
       A large red candle (body > 1.5× prior bar's body) that completely engulfs
       the prior green candle's body, forming at a confirmed resistance zone
       (PDH, prior day's VWAP, upper CPR), with volume > 1.5× 20-day average.
       Short on close of the engulfing bar. Stop above the engulfing bar's high.
       Target: nearest intraday support (VPOC or lower CPR).

  DUAL-DIRECTION PATTERNS (fire +1 or -1 depending on context — two new strategies
  added because they cover signal types genuinely absent from the existing 22):

  S9.  PIN-BAR (Hammer / Shooting Star):
       A single candle with a body occupying ≤ 30% of the bar range and a wick
       ≥ 2× the body length on one side — indicating price rejection at a level.
       Hammer (lower wick): forms at intraday support or VWAP → +1 BUY signal.
       Shooting star (upper wick): forms at intraday resistance or VWAP → -1 SHORT.
       Requires volume > 1.2× 20-bar average to confirm institutional participation.
       Why it's missing from existing 22: all current strategies use multi-bar logic
       (crossovers, bands, breakouts). PIN-BAR is the only single-candle rejection
       pattern. It is among the most widely used signals by Indian intraday traders.

  S10. INTRADAY-STRUCT (Higher Highs / Lower Highs structure):
       After 9:30 AM, evaluate the last 4 pivot highs and lows on 5-min chart.
       HHHL (Higher High Higher Low): intraday uptrend confirmed → BUY pullbacks
              to prior swing low. Stop below last HL. Target: prior HH + range.
       LHLL (Lower High Lower Low): intraday downtrend confirmed → SHORT bounces
              to prior swing high. Stop above last LH. Target: prior LL - range.
       Requires at least 3 confirmed pivot points after open (i.e., fire no earlier
       than 10:15 AM to allow structure to form).
       Why it's missing from existing 22: all current strategies are indicator-based
       (VIX, RSI, MACD, bands). None explicitly tracks price structure — the sequence
       of highs and lows that defines trend direction on the 5-min chart.

Total strategies: 22 existing (repurposed for both directions) + 10 new = 32

Note on strategy counts for quality filter:
  "At least 4 strategies agree" — same threshold for both LONG and SHORT.
  A -1 from VWAP-REV counts as 1 short agreement. A +1 from PIN-BAR counts as 1 long.
  New strategies S1–S10 count toward agreement only if win% >= 50% in learning phase.

### INDEPENDENT LONG/SHORT WEIGHTS

CURRENT: Each strategy has ONE weight used for both +1 and -1 signals.
         RSI-EXT weight = 0.19 (suppressed) applies equally to buy and sell.

PROBLEM: RSI-EXT may be excellent at identifying oversold reversals (buy side)
         but poor at catching overbought tops (sell side), or vice versa.
         Using one weight for both directions hides this asymmetry.

FIX: Each strategy gets two weights — weight_long and weight_short.
  strategy_weights.json structure changes from:
    { "VOL-SPIKE": 2.1, "VWAP-REV": 0.19 ... }
  to:
    { "VOL-SPIKE": {"long": 2.1, "short": 1.4}, "VWAP-REV": {"long": 0.19, "short": 0.8} ... }

  weight_long  updated from outcomes of direction=+1 trades
  weight_short updated from outcomes of direction=-1 trades
  Both start at 1.0 at the beginning of 2016 and evolve independently

Long composite score  = Σ [ (signal=+1 only) × weight_long × regime_modifier ]
Short composite score = Σ [ |signal=-1 only| × weight_short × regime_modifier ]

The score with higher magnitude determines the day's trade direction.
If long_score = 8.5 and short_score = 11.2 → take the short.

================================================================================
### CONFLICT RESOLUTION — WHEN BOTH BUY AND SELL SIGNALS FIRE

Case 1: SAME STOCK has both buy and sell signals simultaneously.
  Example: MACD gives a buy (recent golden cross) but VWAP-REV also gives a
  sell (price 2% above VWAP). These are CONFLICTING strategies on one stock.
  Resolution: Composite score naturally handles this. If buy strategies score
  +3.2 and sell strategies score -2.8, net composite = +0.4 → too low to
  recommend (< threshold of 6.0 required by quality filter). Stock is SKIPPED.
  No manual conflict logic needed.

Case 2: DIFFERENT STOCKS — best LONG candidate and best SHORT candidate both
  pass all quality filters on the same day.
  Resolution: 1 trade/day rule applies. Take the one with higher composite
  score (absolute value). If short_best_score = 9.2 and long_best_score = 7.1,
  take the short on that stock.

Case 3: Market is strongly bullish (Nifty > +1.5%) and short signals appear.
  Apply a regime bias modifier to the composite score selection:
    Nifty > +1.5% up:  long_score × 1.2 (tilt toward longs)
    Nifty < -1.5% down: short_score × 1.2 (tilt toward shorts)
    VIX > 20:          short_score × 1.3 (high VIX amplifies short edge)
  This creates a natural market-direction alignment without hard blocking rules.
  A truly exceptional short signal (score > 10) can still fire on a green day.

### SHORT-SPECIFIC QUALITY FILTERS (additions to existing 7 filters)

Filter 8 — Lower Circuit Guard:
  If stock is at or within 2% of its lower circuit limit, SKIP.
  Reason: if the trade works and you're short, you need to BUY BACK to cover.
  A stock at lower circuit has no sellers willing to give you exit — you're trapped.
  Check: lower_circuit = prev_close × 0.90 (NSE uses 10% circuit bands typically)
         if current_price <= lower_circuit × 1.02 → skip short

Filter 9 — Short Not Against 52-Week Low:
  Do NOT short a stock that is already within 5% of its 52-week low AND has
  Nifty 50 up > 0.5% on the same day.
  Reason: stocks near their lows on a good Nifty day attract buy-the-dip
  institutions. A short here fights institutional buying. The bounce risk is high.
  Exception: allow the short if 5+ strategies agree (extraordinary consensus).

Filter 10 — Corporate Event Blackout:
  Do NOT short a stock if:
    - Tomorrow is the ex-dividend date (dividend-driven buying causes squeeze)
    - Results announcement is within next 2 trading days (gap-up risk overnight)
  Source: this data will need to be fetched from NSE announcements page or
  Kite Connect corporate events API. Mark as TODO for Phase 2B implementation.
  If event data unavailable, use a looser heuristic: skip shorting on
  stocks that moved >5% in prior 3 days (news-driven, unpredictable).

### BACKTESTING METHODOLOGY — ANCHORED WALK-FORWARD (PRIMARY)

The fixed train/test split (2016–2022 train, 2023–2026 test) is NOT sufficient
before deploying real capital. It produces exactly ONE out-of-sample measurement.
If those 3 years happen to suit the strategy by luck, confidence is false.

PRIMARY METHOD: Anchored Walk-Forward Validation (5 independent test windows)

  ┌────────────┬────────────────┬───────────┬────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  WF Run    │   Train On     │  Test On  │  What This Year Validates          │
  ├────────────┼────────────────┼───────────┼────────────────────────────────────┤
  │  WF-1      │  2016–2018     │   2019    │  Late bull market, sideways grind  │
  │  WF-2      │  2016–2019     │   2020    │  COVID crash + V-shaped recovery   │
  │  WF-3      │  2016–2020     │   2021    │  Strong momentum bull year         │
  │  WF-4      │  2016–2021     │   2022    │  Sustained bear + high VIX         │
  │  WF-5      │  2016–2022     │ 2023–2026 │  Post-bear recovery + live period  │
  └────────────┴────────────────┴───────────┴────────────────────────────────────┘

  In each WF run:
    1. Train sequentially (no look-ahead, adaptive weights updated every 20 days)
    2. At the train-end year boundary: freeze weights → save as frozen_YYYY.json
    3. Run the test year in TEST mode (frozen weights, no updates)
    4. Record out-of-sample P&L, win rate, profit factor for that test window
    5. Unfreeze and resume training the next year (next WF run)

  Implementation: year-by-year MANUAL TRIGGERS — same workflow as Phase 1/2.
  scripts/wf_freeze.py is a lightweight snapshot utility (not a full orchestrator):
    python scripts/wf_freeze.py --window N
  This copies current strategy_weights.json → wf{N}_weights.json (~1 second).
  run_testing.py gains a --wf-window N flag to load frozen weights for that test year.
  Checkpoint system handles resume between sessions exactly as before.

GATE TO LIVE DEPLOYMENT:
  Positive P&L in at least 4 of 5 WF windows.
  If WF-2 (2020 COVID) fails → add VIX circuit breaker filter before going live.
  If only 2–3 windows are positive → strategy set needs revision; do NOT go live.
  The WF-5 window (2023–2026) is the most important; the others build confidence.

WHAT EACH DAY'S SCAN DOES (same across all WF runs):
  1. Long-side scan: identifies best LONG candidate (highest long composite score)
  2. Short-side scan: identifies best SHORT candidate (highest |short composite score|)
  3. Direction picker: whichever has higher score wins the day's single trade slot
  4. P&L recorded in paper_trades.csv with direction = LONG or SHORT

WEIGHT LEARNING TIMELINE (same across all WF runs, anchored from 2016):
  - 2016 start: all weight_long = 1.0, all weight_short = 1.0 (32 strategies × 2)
  - Every 20 trading days: update weight_long from +1 trade outcomes,
                            update weight_short from -1 trade outcomes independently
  - At each WF freeze point: save frozen_YYYY.json, switch to TEST mode for 1 year
  - After WF-5 freeze (end of 2022): final deployed weights for live trading

EXPECTED LEARNING FROM HISTORICAL DATA:
  - weight_short for RSI-EXT, VWAP-REV, FAILED-BO will likely dominate
  - weight_long for VOL-SPIKE, ORB-15 will remain highest (confirmed 2016–2018)
  - WF-2 (2020) will likely push weight_short for OPEN-WEAK and DEAD-CAT very high
  - weight_long vs weight_short will differ most for trend strategies (MACD, EMA,
    SUPERTREND) — asymmetric long/short performance in India's structural bull market

### Distributed Agent Memory (key architectural requirement)

                            earnings reaction patterns, macro event effects
  recommender_agent memory: which past recommendations worked and why,
                            stock profile characteristics that lead to wins,
                            time-of-day and day-of-week patterns
  critique_agent memory:    common failure modes, conditions that killed
                            past trades, risk patterns and red flags
  global memory:            cross-agent insights maintained by Manager,
                            overall system performance, regime summaries,
                            lessons that span multiple agents

  This ensures every agent arrives at the daily discussion already informed
  by its own specialised experience, not by a single cluttered shared file.
  See how.txt for the structure of each memory file.

### Agents

1. DATA AGENT: fetches fresh minute data from Kite Connect each morning,
               updates storage, fetches news and FII/DII data. Runs by 8:30 AM.

2. STRATEGY AGENT (formerly Learned Agent): reads its own memory + strategy
               library, scores all 500 stocks using current weights,
               produces ranked candidate list with reasoning.

3. NEWS/SENTIMENT AGENT: analyses overnight corporate announcements, global
               cues, sector news. Flags stocks to avoid or favour.
               Learns and updates its own memory after each market session.

4. RECOMMENDER AGENT: combines Strategy + News outputs, applies all quality
               filters, produces top 5 ranked recommendations with full context.
               Learns from past recommendation outcomes in its own memory.

5. CRITIQUE AGENT: challenges each recommendation — what could go wrong?
               Checks regime mismatch, news risk, overlapping positions.
               Learns failure patterns and red flags in its own memory.

6. MANAGER AGENT: orchestrates the discussion, resolves conflicts, produces
               final morning report before 9:15 AM. After market close,
               reviews outcomes and updates both agent memories and global memory.

### Morning Report Format
  DATE / MARKET BIAS (bullish/bearish/neutral) / VIX / REGIME

  TRADE 1: [SYMBOL]
    Entry zone, Target (+X%), Stop (-Y%), Position size (Rs / shares)
    Risk:Reward ratio, Strategy that triggered, 2-3 sentence reasoning,
    News flags if any.

  DO NOT TRADE IF: [specific conditions that invalidate today's calls]

## LIVE-PRICE ENTRY (Paper Trading Realism)
## Status: DONE — implemented 2026-06-15
================================================================================

### The Problem

Current system flow:
  Bar closes at 13:10 → scan runs → signal found → entry logged at
  strategy's calculated price (e.g., 200.10 from a 9:25 AM bar close).
  But the actual live price at 13:10 is 207.66.

  The paper trade logs entry=200.10, which never existed at 13:10.
  The P&L shown in live_paper_trades.csv is therefore inaccurate.
  This makes paper trading LESS reliable as a predictor of live performance.

  Current workaround: 0.5% drift check — rejects the trade if live price
  has moved >0.5% from strategy entry. This prevents WRONG trades but also
  means some valid setups are rejected just because of the 5-minute scan delay.

### The Ideal Fix

When a signal passes all filters, do NOT use the strategy's calculated
entry price as the paper trade entry. Instead:

  actual_entry   = dm.get_last_price(symbol)       ← current live tick price
  stop_distance  = signal.entry - signal.stop       ← preserve strategy's risk
  target_distance= signal.target - signal.entry     ← preserve strategy's reward
  actual_stop    = actual_entry - stop_distance
  actual_target  = actual_entry + target_distance
  actual_rr      = target_distance / stop_distance  ← R:R is unchanged

This mirrors exactly what a screen-watching trader does:
  - They see the signal on their chart (Camarilla level touched, ORB breakout)
  - They hit BUY at the current market price (not the historical level)
  - Their stop is X rupees below their entry, target is Y rupees above
  - The R:R is the same as the strategy intended

After this fix, the paper trade entry price = live market price = what you
would actually pay. Paper P&L becomes a true simulation of live P&L.

### Will Paper Trading Then Be Same as Watching Screen Live?

For 5-minute strategy trading: YES, essentially.

A human watching the screen:
  1. Watches the 5-min bar close on their chart
  2. Sees the Camarilla/ORB/VWAP condition form
  3. Clicks BUY within 2-3 seconds
  4. Gets filled at the next available price

Our system after this fix:
  1. Bar closes
  2. Scan runs (1-2 seconds for 25 stocks)
  3. Signal identified, all filters checked
  4. Paper trade logged at live tick price (= what you'd pay)

The remaining difference: 1-3 seconds of processing delay vs human click.
On liquid NSE stocks at Rs 5-30L positions, this is < Rs 100 slippage — already
modeled inside net_pnl() via the 0.05% slippage assumption.

What CANNOT be replicated vs a human:
  - Intrabar reactions: human can react mid-bar; system only reacts at bar close.
    For 5-min strategies this is acceptable — signals are designed for bar closes.
  - News awareness: system does not read morning headlines or announcements.
    Phase 3 (News Agent) will address this.
  - Discretion: human can say "market looks weak, skip today." System cannot.
    This is intentional — removes emotional bias.
